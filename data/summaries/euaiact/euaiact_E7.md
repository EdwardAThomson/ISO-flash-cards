# E7: Governance & Penalties

The EU AI Act establishes a comprehensive governance structure and significant penalties for non-compliance.

## Governance Structure

### EU Level

#### European AI Office
Established within the European Commission to:
- Oversee **GPAI model** compliance
- Develop **guidance and guidelines**
- Coordinate with national authorities
- Manage the **EU AI database**
- Support **codes of practice** development
- Monitor AI market developments
- Advise on implementation

#### European Artificial Intelligence Board
Composed of representatives from each Member State to:
- Advise the Commission on AI policy
- Ensure **consistent application** across Member States
- Coordinate between national authorities
- Issue **recommendations and opinions**
- Support harmonized enforcement

#### Advisory Forum
Stakeholder group providing technical expertise:
- Industry representatives
- SMEs and startups
- Civil society
- Academia
- Advises the AI Board and Commission

#### Scientific Panel of Independent Experts
Supports enforcement activities:
- Advises on **GPAI model** capabilities
- Develops evaluation methodologies
- Monitors emerging risks
- Alerts to systemic risks

### National Level

#### National Competent Authorities
Each Member State must designate:
- **Market surveillance authority** — Monitors AI systems on the market
- **Notifying authority** — Designates conformity assessment bodies

#### Market Surveillance
Authorities can:
- Request documentation and information
- Conduct inspections and audits
- Order corrective actions
- Withdraw or recall non-compliant systems
- Impose penalties

---

## EU Database for High-Risk AI Systems

A **public database** maintained by the Commission containing:

| Information | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| Provider details | Identity and contact information |
| System description | Intended purpose and capabilities |
| Conformity status | CE marking and declaration |
| Registration date | When placed on market |
| Modifications | Substantial changes to the system |

**Who must register:**
- Providers of high-risk AI systems
- Providers of GPAI models with systemic risk
- Deployers who are public authorities (or acting on their behalf)

## Penalties

### Prohibited AI Practices
**Most severe violations** — Using or providing prohibited AI systems:
- Up to **€35 million**, or
- **7% of total worldwide annual turnover** (whichever is higher)

### High-Risk AI Non-Compliance
Failure to meet high-risk requirements:
- Up to **€15 million**, or
- **3% of total worldwide annual turnover**

### Incorrect Information
Supplying incorrect, incomplete, or misleading information to authorities:
- Up to **€7.5 million**, or
- **1.5% of total worldwide annual turnover**

### SME & Startup Considerations
For **SMEs and startups**, the lower of the two amounts applies (fixed amount vs. turnover percentage).

### Factors in Determining Penalties

Authorities consider:
- Nature, gravity, and duration of the infringement
- Intentional or negligent character
- Actions taken to mitigate harm
- Degree of cooperation with authorities
- Previous infringements
- Financial benefits gained or losses avoided
- Size and market share of the operator
- Any other aggravating or mitigating factors

---

## Enforcement Mechanisms

### For AI Systems (National Authorities)

1. **Investigation** — Request information, conduct audits
2. **Corrective measures** — Order compliance, modifications
3. **Withdrawal** — Remove from market
4. **Recall** — Retrieve from end users
5. **Penalties** — Administrative fines

### For GPAI Models (AI Office)

1. **Request information** from providers
2. **Conduct evaluations** of models
3. **Request measures** to address systemic risks
4. **Restrict availability** in serious cases
5. **Impose fines** for non-compliance

---

## Complaints & Redress

### Right to Lodge Complaints
Any person can lodge a complaint with a market surveillance authority about:
- Non-compliance with the Act
- Harm caused by AI systems

### Right to Explanation
Affected persons have the right to obtain **meaningful explanations** of decisions made by high-risk AI systems that significantly affect them.

### Judicial Remedies
Affected persons can seek judicial remedies for:
- Decisions of market surveillance authorities
- Harm caused by non-compliant AI systems
