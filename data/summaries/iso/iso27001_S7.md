# Incident Management & Continuity

## Incident Management

Effective incident management minimizes damage and enables rapid recovery from security events.

## Annex A: Incident Management (A.5.24–A.5.28)

### A.5.24 Information Security Incident Management Planning and Preparation

**Control:** Processes and procedures for managing incidents shall be planned and prepared.

### Incident Management Framework

```

│           INCIDENT MANAGEMENT LIFECYCLE                  │
```

### Preparation Elements

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Policies** | Incident management policy and procedures |
| **Roles** | Incident response team, responsibilities |
| **Tools** | Detection, analysis, and response tools |
| **Communication** | Contact lists, escalation paths |
| **Training** | Team training and exercises |
| **Documentation** | Playbooks, checklists, templates |

### A.5.25 Assessment and Decision on Information Security Events

**Control:** Security events shall be assessed and classified as incidents.

### Event vs. Incident

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Event** | Any observable occurrence in a system or network |
| **Incident** | Event that actually or potentially jeopardizes CIA |

### Incident Classification

| Severity | Criteria | Response |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Critical** | Major business impact, widespread | Immediate, all hands |
| **High** | Significant impact, limited scope | Urgent, dedicated team |
| **Medium** | Moderate impact, contained | Timely, normal process |
| **Low** | Minor impact, isolated | Scheduled, standard handling |

### A.5.26 Response to Information Security Incidents

**Control:** Incidents shall be responded to according to procedures.

### Response Activities

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|------------|
| **Contain** | Isolate affected systems, prevent spread |
| **Eradicate** | Remove threat, close vulnerabilities |
| **Recover** | Restore systems, verify integrity |
| **Communicate** | Notify stakeholders, regulators |

### A.5.27 Learning from Information Security Incidents

**Control:** Knowledge from incidents shall be used to strengthen controls.

### Post-Incident Activities

- Root cause analysis
- Lessons learned documentation
- Control improvements
- Process updates
- Training updates
- Metrics tracking

### A.5.28 Collection of Evidence

**Control:** Procedures for evidence collection shall be established.

### Evidence Handling

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Identification** | Recognize potential evidence |
| **Collection** | Gather without alteration |
| **Preservation** | Protect from modification |
| **Chain of custody** | Document handling |
| **Analysis** | Examine without altering original |
| **Reporting** | Document findings |

## Business Continuity

### A.5.29 Information Security During Disruption

**Control:** Plans shall maintain security during disruption.

**Considerations:**
- Security controls during crisis
- Temporary access arrangements
- Alternative processing
- Communication security
- Recovery security

### A.5.30 ICT Readiness for Business Continuity

**Control:** ICT readiness shall be planned, implemented, maintained, and tested.

### Business Continuity Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **BIA** | Business Impact Analysis — identifies critical processes |
| **RTO** | Recovery Time Objective — maximum acceptable downtime |
| **RPO** | Recovery Point Objective — maximum acceptable data loss |
| **MTPD** | Maximum Tolerable Period of Disruption |

### Recovery Strategies

| Strategy | Description | RTO |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| **Hot site** | Fully operational standby | Minutes |
| **Warm site** | Partially configured standby | Hours |
| **Cold site** | Basic facility, no equipment | Days |
| **Cloud DR** | Cloud-based recovery | Variable |
| **Backup/restore** | Restore from backups | Hours to days |

## Compliance & Legal

### A.5.31 Legal, Statutory, Regulatory and Contractual Requirements

**Control:** Requirements shall be identified, documented, and kept up to date.

**Requirement sources:**
- Laws and regulations (GDPR, HIPAA, SOX)
- Industry standards (PCI DSS)
- Contractual obligations
- Organizational policies

### A.5.32 Intellectual Property Rights

**Control:** Procedures to protect intellectual property shall be implemented.

**IPR considerations:**
- Software licensing compliance
- Copyright protection
- Patent awareness
- Trade secret protection

### A.5.33 Protection of Records

**Control:** Records shall be protected from loss, destruction, and falsification.

**Record management:**
- Retention schedules
- Secure storage
- Access controls
- Disposal procedures

### A.5.34 Privacy and Protection of PII

**Control:** Privacy and PII protection shall be ensured as required.

**Privacy principles:**
- Lawful processing
- Purpose limitation
- Data minimization
- Accuracy
- Storage limitation
- Security
- Accountability

### A.5.35 Independent Review of Information Security

**Control:** The approach to managing security shall be independently reviewed.

**Review types:**
- Internal audit
- External audit
- Penetration testing
- Certification audit

### A.5.36 Compliance with Policies, Rules and Standards

**Control:** Compliance with policies and standards shall be regularly reviewed.

### A.5.37 Documented Operating Procedures

**Control:** Operating procedures shall be documented and available.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Prepare before incidents** — Plans, teams, tools ready
2. **Classify appropriately** — Response matches severity
3. **Contain quickly** — Limit damage and spread
4. **Learn from incidents** — Improve controls and processes
5. **Preserve evidence** — May be needed for legal action
6. **Plan for continuity** — Know your RTOs and RPOs
7. **Test regularly** — Plans only work if tested
