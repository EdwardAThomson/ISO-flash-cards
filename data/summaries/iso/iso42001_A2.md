# A2: Context & Stakeholders

## Understanding the Organization and Its Context (Clause 4)

Before implementing an AIMS, organizations must understand the **internal and external factors** that affect their ability to achieve intended outcomes.

## External Context

### Regulatory Environment

| Factor | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **AI-specific regulations** | EU AI Act, national AI laws |
| **Data protection** | GDPR, CCPA, sector-specific rules |
| **Industry regulations** | Healthcare, finance, automotive |
| **International standards** | ISO standards, industry frameworks |

### Market and Technology

| Factor | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Customer expectations** | Transparency, fairness, reliability |
| **Competitive landscape** | Industry AI adoption, best practices |
| **Technology trends** | New AI capabilities, emerging risks |
| **Supply chain** | AI component availability, vendor practices |

### Social and Ethical

| Factor | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Public perception** | Trust in AI, media coverage |
| **Ethical norms** | Societal values, cultural considerations |
| **Workforce impact** | Employment effects, skill requirements |
| **Environmental concerns** | AI energy consumption, sustainability |

## Internal Context

### Organizational Factors

| Factor | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Strategy** | Business objectives, AI strategy alignment |
| **Culture** | Risk appetite, innovation mindset, ethics |
| **Capabilities** | Technical skills, resources, infrastructure |
| **Governance** | Decision-making structures, accountability |

### Existing Systems

| Factor | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Management systems** | ISO 27001, ISO 9001, existing policies |
| **IT infrastructure** | Data systems, computing resources |
| **Processes** | Development methodologies, change management |
| **Documentation** | Existing policies, procedures, records |

## Interested Parties (Stakeholders)

Organizations must identify parties with interests in the AIMS and understand their requirements.

### Internal Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Typical Requirements |
|-------------|---------------------|
| **Executive leadership** | Strategic alignment, risk management, ROI |
| **AI development teams** | Clear requirements, resources, guidance |
| **Operations staff** | Usable systems, training, support |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Regulatory compliance, liability management |
| **HR** | Workforce planning, training, ethics |

### External Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Typical Requirements |
|-------------|---------------------|
| **Customers** | Reliable, fair, transparent AI systems |
| **Regulators** | Compliance, documentation, cooperation |
| **Suppliers/Partners** | Clear requirements, fair contracts |
| **Affected individuals** | Fair treatment, privacy, recourse |
| **Society/Public** | Responsible AI, no harm, transparency |

### Determining Relevant Requirements

For each stakeholder, identify:
1. **Who they are** — Specific identification
2. **What they need** — Their requirements and expectations
3. **Relevance** — Which requirements apply to the AIMS
4. **Obligations** — Legal, contractual, or voluntary commitments

## Scope of the AIMS (Clause 4.3)

### Defining Scope

The scope must consider:
- External and internal issues (Clause 4.1)
- Interested party requirements (Clause 4.2)
- Interfaces and dependencies with other organizations

### Scope Boundaries

| Dimension | Considerations |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Organizational** | Which business units, departments, teams |
| **Geographic** | Which locations, jurisdictions |
| **Technical** | Which AI systems, applications, use cases |
| **Lifecycle** | Which phases (development, deployment, operation) |

### Documenting Scope

The scope statement should be:
- **Clear** — Unambiguous boundaries
- **Complete** — All relevant AI activities included
- **Justified** — Rationale for exclusions (if any)
- **Available** — Accessible to interested parties

### Example Scope Statement

> "The AIMS covers the development, deployment, and operation of machine learning systems used for customer service automation and fraud detection within the Digital Services Division, operating from headquarters and the European data center."

## AI Management System (Clause 4.4)

### Establishing the AIMS

Organizations must establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve an AIMS that includes:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Processes** | Activities needed to manage AI responsibly |
| **Interactions** | How processes relate to each other |
| **Resources** | What's needed to operate processes |
| **Responsibilities** | Who is accountable for what |
| **Documentation** | Policies, procedures, records |

### Process Approach

The AIMS should use a process approach:
1. **Identify processes** needed for the AIMS
2. **Determine sequence** and interaction
3. **Define criteria** for effective operation
4. **Ensure resources** are available
5. **Assign responsibilities** and authorities
6. **Address risks** and opportunities
7. **Evaluate** and improve processes

## Practical Implementation

### Context Analysis Methods

| Method | Use |
|--------|-----|
| **PESTLE analysis** | External factors (Political, Economic, Social, Technical, Legal, Environmental) |
| **SWOT analysis** | Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats |
| **Stakeholder mapping** | Identify and prioritize stakeholders |
| **Gap analysis** | Current state vs. requirements |

### Stakeholder Engagement

- **Identify** all relevant stakeholders
- **Analyze** their interests and influence
- **Prioritize** based on impact and importance
- **Engage** through appropriate channels
- **Document** requirements and commitments
- **Review** regularly as context changes

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Context is foundational** — Understanding environment shapes the AIMS
2. **Stakeholders matter** — Their requirements drive system design
3. **Scope must be clear** — Ambiguity creates compliance gaps
4. **Documentation is required** — Scope and context must be recorded
5. **Regular review** — Context and stakeholders change over time
