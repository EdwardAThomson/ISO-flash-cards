# Leadership & Policy

## Leadership Commitment (Clause 5.1)

Top management must demonstrate **leadership and commitment** to the ISMS.

### Management Responsibilities

| Responsibility | Actions |
|----------------|---------|
| **Policy & objectives** | Establish and align with strategic direction |
| **Integration** | Embed ISMS into business processes |
| **Resources** | Ensure adequate resources are available |
| **Communication** | Promote importance of information security |
| **Results** | Ensure ISMS achieves intended outcomes |
| **Direction** | Guide and support people to contribute |
| **Improvement** | Promote continual improvement |
| **Support** | Enable other managers to demonstrate leadership |

### Evidence of Commitment

- Active participation in ISMS activities
- Security topics in management meetings
- Resource allocation decisions
- Visible support for security initiatives
- Recognition of security achievements
- Personal compliance with policies

## Information Security Policy (Clause 5.2)

### Policy Requirements

Top management must establish an information security policy that:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Appropriate** | Suitable to the organization's purpose |
| **Framework** | Provides basis for setting objectives |
| **Commitment** | Includes commitment to satisfy requirements |
| **Improvement** | Includes commitment to continual improvement |

### Policy Content

A comprehensive policy typically includes:

#### Core Elements
- Purpose and scope
- Definition of information security
- Management commitment
- Principles and objectives
- Compliance requirements
- Consequences of violations
- Review and update process

#### Supporting Statements
- Roles and responsibilities
- Risk management approach
- Asset classification
- Access control principles
- Incident management
- Business continuity
- Training and awareness

### Policy Communication

The policy must be:
- **Documented** — Available as documented information
- **Communicated** — Known within the organization
- **Available** — Accessible to interested parties as appropriate

## Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities (Clause 5.3)

### Assignment Requirements

Top management must ensure that responsibilities and authorities are:
- **Assigned** — Clearly allocated to roles
- **Communicated** — Known to relevant parties

### Key Roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Top Management** | Overall accountability, policy, resources |
| **ISMS Manager** | Day-to-day management, coordination |
| **Risk Owner** | Accountability for specific risks |
| **Asset Owner** | Accountability for information assets |
| **Process Owner** | Accountability for ISMS processes |
| **Internal Auditor** | Independent assessment |

### Required Authorities

Top management must assign responsibility and authority for:

1. **Ensuring conformity** — ISMS conforms to ISO 27001 requirements
2. **Reporting performance** — ISMS performance reported to top management

### RACI Matrix

A useful tool for clarifying responsibilities:

| Activity | Top Mgmt | ISMS Mgr | Risk Owner | IT |
|----------|----------|----------|------------|-----|
| Approve policy | A | R | C | I |
| Risk assessment | A | R | R | C |
| Control implementation | I | A | C | R |
| Internal audit | A | C | I | I |
| Management review | R/A | R | C | I |

*R = Responsible, A = Accountable, C = Consulted, I = Informed*

## Annex A: Organizational Controls (A.5)

### A.5.1 Policies for Information Security

**Control:** Information security policy and topic-specific policies shall be defined, approved, published, communicated, and acknowledged.

**Implementation:**
- Develop policy hierarchy
- Obtain management approval
- Publish through appropriate channels
- Communicate to all personnel
- Obtain acknowledgment
- Review periodically

### A.5.2 Information Security Roles and Responsibilities

**Control:** Information security roles and responsibilities shall be defined and allocated.

**Implementation:**
- Define roles in job descriptions
- Document in ISMS procedures
- Communicate to role holders
- Include in contracts

### A.5.3 Segregation of Duties

**Control:** Conflicting duties and responsibilities shall be segregated.

**Purpose:** Reduce opportunity for unauthorized or unintentional modification or misuse.

**Examples:**
- Separate development from production access
- Separate request from approval
- Separate custody from record-keeping

### A.5.4 Management Responsibilities

**Control:** Management shall require all personnel to apply information security in accordance with policies.

**Implementation:**
- Include security in job descriptions
- Set security objectives
- Provide resources and training
- Monitor compliance
- Address non-compliance

## Annex A: People Controls (A.6)

### A.6.1 Screening

**Control:** Background verification checks shall be carried out on candidates.

**Considerations:**
- Legal and regulatory requirements
- Role sensitivity
- Privacy regulations
- Proportionality

### A.6.2 Terms and Conditions of Employment

**Control:** Employment contracts shall state responsibilities for information security.

**Include:**
- Confidentiality obligations
- Acceptable use requirements
- Compliance with policies
- Consequences of violations
- Post-employment obligations

### A.6.3 Information Security Awareness, Education and Training

**Control:** Personnel shall receive appropriate awareness, education, and training.

**Program elements:**
- Induction training
- Regular awareness updates
- Role-specific training
- Phishing simulations
- Policy acknowledgment

### A.6.4 Disciplinary Process

**Control:** A disciplinary process shall be established for security violations.

**Process elements:**
- Clear definition of violations
- Investigation procedures
- Proportionate responses
- Documentation requirements
- Appeal mechanisms

### A.6.5 Responsibilities After Termination or Change

**Control:** Security responsibilities that remain valid after termination shall be defined and enforced.

**Include:**
- Confidentiality obligations
- Non-disclosure agreements
- Return of assets
- Access revocation

## Key Takeaways

1. **Leadership is essential** — Top management must actively support the ISMS
2. **Policy sets direction** — Clear, communicated, and enforced
3. **Roles must be clear** — Everyone knows their responsibilities
4. **Segregation reduces risk** — Separate conflicting duties
5. **People are key** — Screening, training, and awareness matter
