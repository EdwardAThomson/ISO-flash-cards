# N2: GOVERN Function

The **GOVERN** function establishes the foundation for AI risk management by creating a culture of responsibility, defining policies, and ensuring accountability across the organization.

## Purpose

GOVERN ensures that:
- AI risk management is embedded in organizational culture
- Clear policies and procedures guide AI development and use
- Roles and responsibilities are defined
- Resources are allocated appropriately
- Continuous improvement is supported

## GOVERN Categories

### GOVERN 1: Policies, Processes, Procedures, and Practices

**GV 1.1** — Legal and regulatory requirements are understood and managed
- Identify applicable laws and regulations
- Monitor regulatory developments
- Ensure compliance mechanisms

**GV 1.2** — AI risk management processes are established
- Define risk tolerance levels
- Establish risk assessment procedures
- Create escalation pathways

**GV 1.3** — Processes are in place for continuous improvement
- Regular policy reviews
- Lessons learned integration
- Feedback mechanisms

**GV 1.4** — Risk management processes are transparent
- Document decision-making rationale
- Enable stakeholder understanding
- Support audit and review

### GOVERN 2: Accountability Structures

**GV 2.1** — Roles and responsibilities are defined
- Executive sponsorship
- Risk management ownership
- Technical accountability
- Operational responsibility

**GV 2.2** — Mechanisms exist to supersede or override AI decisions
- Human-in-the-loop processes
- Appeal mechanisms
- Emergency stop procedures

**GV 2.3** — Executive leadership is engaged
- Board-level awareness
- Resource commitment
- Strategic alignment

### GOVERN 3: Workforce Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Accessibility

**GV 3.1** — Diverse perspectives inform AI development
- Inclusive team composition
- Stakeholder engagement
- Community input

**GV 3.2** — Accessibility is considered
- Design for diverse users
- Accommodate disabilities
- Consider digital literacy

### GOVERN 4: Organizational Culture

**GV 4.1** — Culture supports AI risk management
- Psychological safety for raising concerns
- Incentives aligned with responsible AI
- Leadership modeling

**GV 4.2** — Feedback mechanisms exist
- Incident reporting channels
- Whistleblower protections
- Continuous feedback loops

**GV 4.3** — Awareness and training programs
- Role-appropriate training
- Regular updates
- Competency assessment

### GOVERN 5: Stakeholder Engagement

**GV 5.1** — Relevant stakeholders are identified
- Internal stakeholders (employees, leadership)
- External stakeholders (users, affected communities)
- Regulators and oversight bodies

**GV 5.2** — Stakeholder feedback is incorporated
- Consultation processes
- Impact assessments
- Grievance mechanisms

### GOVERN 6: Policies and Procedures

**GV 6.1** — Policies address AI lifecycle stages
- Design and development
- Testing and validation
- Deployment and monitoring
- Decommissioning

**GV 6.2** — Policies address third-party AI
- Vendor assessment
- Contractual requirements
- Ongoing monitoring

## Key Governance Elements

### AI Risk Management Policy

A comprehensive policy should address:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Scope** | Which AI systems are covered |
| **Principles** | Guiding values for AI use |
| **Roles** | Who is responsible for what |
| **Processes** | How risks are identified and managed |
| **Oversight** | How compliance is monitored |
| **Review** | How often policies are updated |

### Governance Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Board / Executive           │
│    (Strategic oversight, policy)    │
└─────────────────┬───────────────────┘
                  │
┌─────────────────▼───────────────────┐
│      AI Risk Committee / Council    │
│   (Cross-functional coordination)   │
└─────────────────┬───────────────────┘
                  │
┌─────────────────▼───────────────────┐
│        Operational Teams            │
│  (Development, deployment, ops)     │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Documentation Requirements

- AI system inventory
- Risk assessments
- Decision logs
- Incident reports
- Audit trails
- Training records

## Implementation Tips

1. **Start with leadership buy-in** — Governance requires executive commitment
2. **Integrate with existing frameworks** — Build on current risk management
3. **Make it practical** — Policies should be actionable, not theoretical
4. **Enable feedback** — Create safe channels for raising concerns
5. **Review regularly** — Update as technology and context evolve

---

## Key Takeaways

- GOVERN is the **foundation** for all other AI RMF functions
- Effective governance requires **clear accountability** at all levels
- **Culture matters** — policies alone are insufficient
- **Stakeholder engagement** improves outcomes and builds trust
